My Background / About Me
I have a unique background that blends historical research with data science. With a BA and MA in History, my research focused on trade, census records, and economic trends in early America—requiring extensive data analysis, critical thinking, and strong technical writing skills. My ability to synthesize complex historical datasets into clear, compelling narratives naturally translated into data science, leading me to earn an MS in the field.
My tech stack includes Python (Pandas, NumPy, Scikit-Learn, StatsModels, TensorFlow), R (Tidyverse, Forecast), SQL, FastAPI, Flask, Docker, and Google Earth Engine (GEE) for geospatial analysis. I have built predictive models for revenue forecasting, conducted A/B testing with logistic regression, developed recommender systems, and deployed fraud detection models using CI/CD pipelines.
Beyond technical expertise, my experience as a historian sharpened my ability to distill complex findings into well-structured reports, craft data-driven narratives, and communicate insights effectively—a skill set that is essential in the data science field. Whether writing research papers, technical documentation, or stakeholder reports, I excel at transforming raw data into meaningful stories that drive decision-making. I’m passionate about leveraging both my analytical and communication skills to uncover actionable insights and bridge the gap between data and business strategy.
